```markdown
Total light, \( E_0 = \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} E(\theta, \phi) \sin \theta \cos \theta \, d\theta \, d\phi \)

Light received, \( L = L(\theta_e, \phi_e) \)

\( L(\theta_e, \phi_e) = \int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} f(\theta, \phi) g_e(\phi_e) E(\theta, \phi) \sin \theta \cos \theta \, d\theta \, d\phi \)

(⚠️ lens is considered as a pinhole
→ does not work for a microscope!)

To get albedo: integrate BRDF over \((\theta_e, \phi_e)\)

- Ideal Lambertian Surfaces:
  1. reflects all incident light
  2. appears equally bright viewed from all directions

1. ⇒ albedo = 1
2. ⇒ \( f \) constant

\[
\int_{-\pi}^{\pi} \int_{0}^{\pi/2} f E \cos \theta_e \sin \theta \cos \theta \sin \theta \, d\theta \, d\phi = E \cos \theta
\]
```
